Delicious Momo Recipe

  • Ingredients
  • Instructions
  • Tips

Momo is a popular type of steamed or fried dumpling native to Tibet, Nepal, and surrounding regions. Whether you're a beginner or looking to refine your momo-making skills, this recipe will guide you through the process.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Approximately 3/4 cup water

For the Filling:

  • 250 grams minced chicken or vegetables (e.g., cabbage, carrots, onions)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon minced ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro (optional)

For the Sauce (Optional):

  • 2 tomatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt.
    • Gradually add water and knead until you get a smooth and elastic dough.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Make the Filling:
    • In another bowl, combine minced meat or vegetables with soy sauce, garlic, ginger, salt, pepper, and cilantro.
    • Mix well to ensure all ingredients are evenly distributed.
  3. Assemble the Momos:
    • Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls.
    • Roll each ball into a thin circle (about 3 inches in diameter).
    • Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each wrapper.
    • Fold and pleat the edges to seal the momo. You can make half-moon shapes or traditional round pleats.
  4. Cook the Momos:
    • Steaming Method:
      • Place momos in a steamer lined with parchment paper or lightly greased to prevent sticking.
      • Steam for about 15-20 minutes until the dough is cooked and slightly translucent.
    • Frying Method (Fried Momos):
      • Heat oil in a pan.
      • Place momos in the pan and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Prepare the Sauce (Optional):
    • Blend boiled tomatoes with dried red chilies and sesame seeds.
    • Add soy sauce and salt to taste.
    • Serve the sauce alongside the momos.
  6. Serve:
    • Enjoy the momos hot with the prepared sauce or your favorite chutney.

Tips for Perfect Momos

  • Dough Consistency: Ensure the dough is smooth and elastic to make it easier to roll and pleat.
  • Filling Moisture: The filling should be moist but not too wet to prevent the momo wrappers from becoming soggy.
  • Sealing: Properly seal the momos to avoid the filling from leaking during cooking.
  • Cooking Time: Adjust steaming time based on the size of the momos to ensure they are thoroughly cooked.
  • Variations: Experiment with different fillings like paneer, tofu, or mixed vegetables for variety.
